Linus Pauling's Work on Molecular Structure: A Legacy of Discovery

The Relentless Defender of Peace: Linus Pauling

Linus Pauling is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century, particularly for his groundbreaking contributions to the understanding of molecular structure. One of his most famous discoveries was the alpha-helix structure of proteins, which revolutionized molecular biology and laid the foundation for our understanding of how proteins fold and function. Pauling’s research also expanded to the structure of hemoglobin, the molecule responsible for carrying oxygen in red blood cells, and he was instrumental in the discovery of how genetic diseases like sickle cell anemia are linked to molecular abnormalities. His insights into chemical bonding and the way molecules interact within living organisms reshaped both chemistry and biochemistry, making him a pivotal figure in modern science.

Did you know?

  • The molecular complementarity concept suggests that molecules interact by fitting together like a “hand in a glove,” which revolutionized our understanding of how molecules recognize and interact with each other.
  • Linus Pauling’s Valence Bond Theory shows that electrons can be shared between atoms in more complex ways than previously thought.
